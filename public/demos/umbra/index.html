Chapter I — The Creed

When the sun leaves the sky, appetite begins.

Manifesto — 001

Umbra is a restaurant above the clouds — twenty-four chairs on a volcanic ridge, higher than the weather. We cook with fire and its absence: ember, ash, smoke, and the cold black of the lava caves beneath the dining room. One seating a night. It begins at dusk and ends under the Milky Way.

Chapter IV — Its Absence

By Dark

Beneath the room, lava tubes hold thirteen degrees the year round. Cured fish, koji, old cheese, beeswax. What fire doesn’t finish, darkness does.

Chapter IV — Fire

By Fire

A fire that has not gone out since opening night. Vine cuttings, fig wood, pine from the caldera. It roasts, it cures, it perfumes — the only clock in our kitchen.

Move to tip the balance

Chapter V — The Hand

Aitana Roque

“I don’t plate food. I plate the hour.”

Born on the island, trained at fire temples in the Basque hills and pastry rooms in Paris, Roque returned to the mountain that made her. Her kitchen keeps one law: nothing reaches the table that hasn’t met fire, ash, or darkness.

Born Garafía, 1987 · Fire since 2011 · Two stars, kept lightly

— A. Roque
