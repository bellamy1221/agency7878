The Restaurant — Cap d'en Font

The island wakes east. Levante is named for the wind that carries the morning.

On Menorca's south coast, a single shelf of marès limestone holds twelve tables above the water. The fire is lit at noon. Lunch begins at half past one and follows the light until it leaves — five hours, five acts, nothing hurried. Nothing on the table has travelled further than the island's shore.

The House — Architecture by Estudi Migjorn

A quarry, reopened.

For five centuries Menorca built itself from marès — the pale stone cut from its own ground. Levante returns to a disused quarry face above the water: one room, one long shelf of stone, finished in island oak, raw linen and cast bronze. The sun does the decorating.

The Kitchen

Marta Riudavets

We don't cook against the clock. We cook with the light.

Marta cooked over Basque fires for a decade before the island called her home. At Levante she keeps a short menu and a long fire, and lets the day decide the rest.
